How We Work

The process,
from hello to home.

Every interior design project with Cotswold House Co. follows a considered path. No surprises, no mass-market shortcuts — just a careful, collaborative route from first conversation to the moment you step into your finished home.

  1. i

    Discovery

    A conversation about your home, how you live in it, and the story you want it to tell.

  2. ii

    Concept

    Palette, mood, plan. A considered direction grounded in classic British sensibility.

  3. iii

    Specify

    Materials, finishes, furniture, and lighting. Sourced from trusted UK and regional suppliers across the Carolinas.

  4. iv

    Build

    Close coordination with local builders and craftsmen across the Carolinas. We hold the detail so you don't have to.

  5. v

    Style

    Final layering — soft furnishings, artwork, the small English pieces that make it yours.

Our Style

Clean lines,
calm, considered.

Our style is clean lines, calm, spa-like spaces, with purposeful design to hide every-day items. Elegance and charm with a nod of character — without being over-powering or floral.

Textures, layers, interest through design. Rather than shouting through big patterns.

The reference points are English and deliberately short. Daylesford and Lady Bamford. The Wild Rabbit in Kingham. Soho Farmhouse out near Great Tew. 11 Bibury. And the Cotswold stone of Woodstock, Chipping Norton, Stow-on-the-Wold, Upper and Lower Slaughter, and Bibury — the market towns and villages that shaped the eye. Unhurried, layered, quietly English. That is the feeling we bring to every home.

The Studio

Dani Wilkinson.

Dani grew up in the Cotswolds — Bibury, Burford, the Cotswold stone villages of Gloucestershire — where houses felt like they had always been there. A layered calm, built through texture rather than pattern. That is the eye she brought with her to Greenville.

From the Cotswolds · To the American South

Old England,
new home.

Bibury — described by William Morris as "the most beautiful village in England" — is where our story begins. The Cotswold stone cottages of Arlington Row, the River Coln winding through the village, the swans gliding across its waters: this is the world that shapes our eye.

And on the right is what that sensibility looks like, translated. Calm, layered, built through texture rather than pattern — not the village itself, but the feeling of it, brought home to a Greenville build.
